Historical Places

Melaka is a state famous for its historical places among locals and foreign visitors. On this page we will show some historical places that are often visited by visitors in Bandar Hilir, Melaka.

The Middelburg Bastion was one of nine bastions of the Fortress of Malacca, in Melaka City, Melaka, Malaysia located at the mouth of Melaka River. The bastion has been restored, together with cannons, and is open for visitors. The Dutch name is in reference to the city of Middelburg, Zeeland.

Middelburg Bastion

The church is a twin-spired neo-gothic structure. It is believed that the church was modeled after the Cathedral of St. Peter in Montpellier, Southern France, which closely followed the older church’s original construction, except for a portico which was added on in 1963 Now this church has become a visitor attraction in recognizing historical places in Melaka.

Church of St. Francis Xavier

Hang Jebat Mausoleum is a burial ground along Jalan Kampung Kuli in Malacca dedicated to the legendary warrior Hang Jebat. Also known as Makam Hang Jebat, the crypt clearly predates the 1512 Portuguese occupation. Visitors can visit at any time as it is open 24 hours a day.

Hang Jebat Mausoleum

Bastion Victoria got its name on November 7, 1641 during the Dutch era. The original name given by the Portuguese was St. Sunday. It served as an important defensive fort for the city of Melaka in the Portuguese and Dutch eras to withstand attacks from the north and east.

Bastion Victoria

Stadthuys which means "city hall" in Dutch is a historic building located next to the Church of Christ in Jalan Laksamana, Melaka, Malaysia. The building was built in 1650 as the official residence of the Dutch Governor and Deputy Governor, where the structure of the building symbolizes the fine art of Dutch design.

The Stadthuys

This memorial building was established in 1912. Malacca Proclamation of Independence Memorial displays records and photographs on the early history of the Malay Sultanate. The building used to house the Malacca Club which was used as the social centre of the British people in British Malaya. The memorial was set up and officiated by former Prime Minister Tunku Abdul Rahman on 31 August 1985, 28 years after the independence of the country.

Proclamation of Independence Memorial

Christ Church built by the Dutch when they took possession of Malacca from the Portuguese, it's one of Malacca’s most defining structures. Situated along Jalan Gereja (also known as Church Street) it is an instantly recognizable brick-red building with a huge white cross at the top.

Christ Church Melaka

Saint Paul's Church is a historic church building in Malacca City, Malaysia that was originally built in 1521, making it the oldest church building in Malaysia and Southeast Asia This building is built on the site of the last Malaccan sultan’s istana (palace), and it was constructed by Portuguese fidalgo (nobleman) captain, Duarte Coelho, in gratitude to the Virgin Mary for saving his life during a storm at sea.

St. Paul Church

Melaka Sultanate Palace is a wooden replica of Sultan Mansur Shah’s 15th-century palace. Set at the base of St. Paul’s Hill it was painstakingly constructed in 1985 using traditional construction techniques and materials, based on accounts in the 16th century This museum is open everyday and the operating time is from 9a.m. to 5p.m.

Melaka Sultanate Palace Museum
